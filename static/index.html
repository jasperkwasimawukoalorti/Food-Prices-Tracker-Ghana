Two views, one dataset: Trader (Wholesale) is what a bulk buyer pays a supplier. Consumer (Retail) is closer to what you'd pay at a market stall \u2014 but every Retail figure in this dataset is WFP's own modeled estimate, not a directly observed price, so it's labeled "estimated" rather than shown the same way as an observed number.

Where this comes from: World Food Programme price reports for Ghana, normalized to price-per-kilogram. Commodities quoted only by count (tubers, bunches, pieces) aren't shown in a view where they lack a valid weight conversion \u2014 coverage genuinely differs between the two views for a few commodities.

On regions: Ghana has had 16 official regions since 2019, but the source data still reports under the 10 older regional boundaries \u2014 this tracker shows exactly those 10, not estimates for the newer six.
